1. Existing Platform Building Blocks (Already Built)

These are the concrete modules this report assumes you will reuse:

  • Pipeline orchestration: src/scraper/run.ts (stage pipeline, retries, status tracking).
  • Parse and score engine: src/scraper/parse.ts, src/scraper/assess.ts, src/scraper/score.ts.
  • Database model: src/db/schema.ts with run snapshots, flags, review state, rewrites, and evidence tables.
  • Explorer and filters: consultant explorer patterns in src/app/consultants.
  • Action centre: prioritized uplift workflow in src/app/actions.
  • Review queue: low-confidence and fail-flag handling in src/app/consultants/review.
  • Copilot: natural-language data querying in src/components/ui/ai-copilot.tsx and src/app/api/copilot/route.ts.
  • Reports: PDF and CSV flows in src/app/reports and export APIs.
  • Configuration controls: score/tier controls in src/app/configuration.
Interpretation rule used below: "What you would build" means a domain-adapted variant of the same architecture pattern: data job(s) -> deterministic + AI scoring -> explorer dashboards -> action queue -> review workflow -> board reporting.

4. Detailed Idea Build Blueprints (What You Would Actually Build)

1) Employer Acquisition and Retention Intelligence Fee: Medium-High

What you would build:

  • Data jobs: ingest employer CRM account data, apprenticeship vacancies, account touchpoint history, and public company sector signals.
  • Scoring model: employer health score (engagement recency, placements filled, apprenticeship outcomes, churn risk indicators).
  • Pages/modules: Employer Explorer (consultant-table pattern), Employer Benchmark (hospital comparison pattern), At-Risk Accounts queue (review queue pattern).
  • Action centre: auto-prioritized actions like "recover inactive employer", "upsell standards", "fix conversion bottlenecks".
  • Copilot/reporting: NL queries for account risk and monthly employer growth board PDF.

Data accessibility: 3/5 (mixed public and internal CRM/MIS data).

Pros
  • Directly linked to employer growth.
  • Clear commercial language.
  • Strong recurring usage by account teams.
Cons / Risks
  • Needs disciplined CRM updates.
  • Cross-team ownership required.
  • Attribution arguments possible.

2) Apprenticeship Pipeline Intelligence Fee: Medium-High

What you would build:

  • Data jobs: vacancy demand feed, application pipeline stages, on-program attendance/progress, completion status by standard/employer.
  • Scoring model: pipeline velocity score + completion risk score + employer demand match score.
  • Pages/modules: Apprenticeship Funnel dashboard, Standards Performance explorer, Employer x Standard heatmap.
  • Action centre: prioritized actions for stalled applicants, high-risk cohorts, and standards with weak completion trend.
  • Exports: weekly operational list (team execution) and monthly executive summary.

Data accessibility: 2/5 (mostly internal data dependencies).

Pros
  • Strong alignment to strategic priority.
  • High operational value.
  • Ties directly to outcomes and funding.
Cons / Risks
  • Multiple internal systems involved.
  • Data quality and ownership needed.
  • Can expose process weaknesses.

3) Skills Demand and Curriculum Radar Fee: Medium

What you would build:

  • Data jobs: pull public labour-market data, local job-posting trends, employer feedback themes, and current enrolment trend inputs.
  • Scoring model: course-market fit score, demand momentum score, and curriculum risk/opportunity ranking.
  • Pages/modules: Curriculum Opportunity Map, Sector Demand board, Course viability list with confidence flags.
  • Action centre: recommendations to expand, reshape, or review low-fit provision.
  • Reports: termly curriculum planning pack and stakeholder narrative.

Data accessibility: 4/5 (public-data-heavy with light internal overlay).

Pros
  • Good strategic value.
  • Public data supports speed.
  • Supports external engagement story.
Cons / Risks
  • Signal lag/noise in market data.
  • Needs careful confidence messaging.
  • Impact can be medium-term.

4) Ofsted Improvement Intelligence Fee: Medium-High

What you would build: quality-theme tracker where each action has owner, due date, evidence, and status; mapped to inspection themes with monthly confidence scoring and governance reporting.

  • Use action and review queue patterns already present in /actions and /consultants/review.
  • Add evidence quality score and unresolved risk flags.
  • Generate monthly quality board PDF and action completion trend charts.

Data accessibility: 3/5 (public framework, internal evidence data).

Pros: urgent and board-visible. Cons: requires strong definitions and change discipline.

5) Attendance and Withdrawal Risk Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: attendance and withdrawal risk engine with cohort/course risk ranking, intervention queue, and resolution tracking.

  • Daily/weekly MIS ingestion job.
  • Risk score rules + AI notes for intervention rationale.
  • Tutor/team worklist export and exception alerts.

Data accessibility: 2/5 (internal-data-heavy).

Pros: strong impact potential. Cons: operational adoption is critical.

6) Course Recruitment Conversion Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: enquiry-to-enrolment funnel intelligence by course/campus/channel with stage loss diagnostics and prioritized corrective actions.

  • Ingest enquiry, application, offer, acceptance, enrolment events.
  • Create stage conversion score and leak detection rules.
  • Dashboard with conversion by channel and course-level action list.

Data accessibility: 2/5 (internal funnel and CRM data).

Pros: commercially understandable. Cons: requires alignment across admissions and marketing workflows.

7) Course Content Quality Scoring and Rewrite Fee: Medium

What you would build: direct extension of existing rewrite stack to course pages: scrape page copy, assess clarity/completeness, propose rewrites with evidence, and project uplift.

  • Reuses rewrite engine components in src/lib/rewrite-engine.ts and evidence model in DB.
  • Adds course-specific scoring dimensions (entry requirements clarity, outcomes clarity, employer relevance).
  • Publishes content improvement queue with owner and status.

Data accessibility: 4/5 (mostly public website content).

Pros: high reuse, fast delivery. Cons: needs editorial governance process.

8) SEND and High-Needs Progression Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: progression intelligence for high-needs learners: support-plan status, intervention effectiveness, and progression outcome tracking.

  • Secure ingestion pipelines for sensitive support/learning records.
  • Risk and progression scoring with explainable flags.
  • Governance dashboard for specialist teams and leadership.

Data accessibility: 2/5 (sensitive internal data).

Pros: high mission alignment. Cons: higher governance/compliance overhead.

9) Work Placement and T Level Capacity Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: placement demand/supply planner with capacity forecasting and at-risk placement alerts by sector and employer.

  • Combine learner placement demand with employer placement capacity feeds.
  • Capacity fit score and forecast gap scoring.
  • Action centre for placement shortfall mitigation.

Data accessibility: 3/5 (mixed internal and external inputs).

Pros: practical operational value. Cons: employer feed freshness can vary.

10) Funding Opportunity and Bid Intelligence Fee: Low-Medium

What you would build: funding-call watcher with strategic-fit scoring, bid/no-bid recommendations, and evidence checklist generator.

  • Public feed ingestion for funding calls and deadlines.
  • Fit score using Kirklees strategic themes and delivery track record.
  • Bid tracker dashboard plus deadline alerts.

Data accessibility: 5/5 (public-data-first).

Pros: low data friction. Cons: usage peaks around bid cycles.

11) Student Destination and Outcomes Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: destination pipeline that links learners to progression outcomes with quality indicators by program and cohort.

  • Ingest completion and destination evidence from internal plus external sources.
  • Outcome quality score and trend movement.
  • Board and curriculum views with confidence markers for missing evidence.

Data accessibility: 3/5 (mixed internal and external).

Pros: strong narrative value. Cons: lag and completeness issues in destination data.

12) Timetable and Room Utilization Intelligence Fee: Medium

What you would build: utilization monitor for room occupancy, scheduling clashes, underused assets, and timetable optimization opportunities.

  • Ingest timetable, attendance, and room metadata feeds.
  • Utilization efficiency score and conflict severity flags.
  • Action queue for rescheduling and estate optimization.

Data accessibility: 2/5 (internal systems dependent).

Pros: strong efficiency angle. Cons: integration and adoption effort required.

13) Competitor Offer Intelligence Fee: Low-Medium

What you would build: regional competitor monitor for course launches, messaging shifts, and target-audience positioning changes.

  • Public website and publication monitoring pipeline (terms-respecting).
  • Differentiation and overlap scoring by subject area.
  • Monthly competitor movement brief.

Data accessibility: 4/5 (mostly public with terms caveats).

Pros: strategic positioning support. Cons: often secondary budget priority.

14) Green Skills Impact Intelligence Fee: Low-Medium

What you would build: impact measurement dashboard tracking green curriculum activity, employer engagement, learner outcomes, and strategic evidence.

  • Public policy/funding feed plus internal program metrics.
  • Green impact scorecards by curriculum area.
  • Exportable evidence packs for stakeholders and bids.

Data accessibility: 4/5 (public-data-friendly plus internal metrics).

Pros: policy alignment. Cons: direct ROI can be less immediate.

15) Reputation and Community Signal Intelligence Fee: Low-Medium

What you would build: monitored signal layer for public sentiment themes, issue spikes, and stakeholder concern alerts.

  • Public signal ingestion with source quality controls.
  • Theme and severity classification with false-positive suppression.
  • Leadership briefing page and incident watchlist export.

Data accessibility: 3/5 (public signals but uneven source constraints).

Pros: early warning value. Cons: noisy signals if not tightly governed.
